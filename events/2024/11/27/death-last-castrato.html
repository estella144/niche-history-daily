Death of the Last Castrato

27 November 1975 - 2024

Alessandro Moreschi, an Italian singer, is often called the "last castrato". He lived from 1858 to 1922.

A castrato is a male singer, who was castrated before puberty, and could therefore retain a high voice, similar to that of a soprano.

After the unification of Italy in 1861, "eviration" (castration) was made illegal. The pope, Pius X, instructed that, instead of using castratos, boys should be used instead.

Moreschi was the only castrato to make solo recordings. It is in this way that the voice of the castrato was preserved.

Moreschi died on 27 November 1975 at the age of 63. He was buried in the Campo Verano in Rome with his colleague, Domenico Salvatori.
