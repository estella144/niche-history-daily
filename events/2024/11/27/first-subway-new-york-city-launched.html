First Subway in New York City Launched

27 November 1904 - 2024

The IRT Ninth Avenue Line was the first elevated railway in New York City. Its opening marked the beginning of the modern subway system.

It was opened for experimental operations in July 1868, but did not begin official operations until 1904. It ran from Battery Place to 155th Street.

On 11 September 1905, a train running on the line derailed and fell into the street, causing 61 casualties.

In June 1940, the line ceased operations, being replaced by the IND Eighth Avenue Line which had opened in 1932.

Today, the A, B, C, D and E lines use the Eighth Avenue Line.
